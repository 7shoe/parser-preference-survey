Setting

The quality of output text from a parser is crucial for us (direct impact on LLM performance). However, there is no universal measure to assess the goodness of parsed text. Surrogate measures like BLEU were created for sentence-length, neural translation; hardly able to handle comparisons of texts spanning dozens of pages or LaTeX. Regardless, faced with two options most people have a good idea what we prefer. DPO (direct preference optimization) allows AI models to fine-tune on these preferences and align models with these user preferences.

Imagine the following situation: You hold a page of your favorite document in your hand that is about to disentigrate. Two parsers provide a good textual guess of its content but you can only pick one. What option do you choose (for whatever reasons you deem reasonable)?

Why is this meaningful?

  • We parse ~1 billion document pages: shouldn't we take a look what comes out?
  • Get a feeling for different parsers, their strengths their failure modes
  • Develop a new metric & help to step away BLEU, ROUGE, etc. that were never ment for long text comparisons
  • Training data for DPO --> it will improve our parsing quality!
  • Test data for evaluation --> see were we are and what improvements can be made that are highly valued by users
  • IPDPS deadline: October 3rd 2024 :-)

Advice

  • Be principled but follow your gut (preferences are not fixed rules; otherwise we could code them up)
  • Don't read but skim (instead check subsection titles, paragraph beginnings, exotic words)
  • Consider in advance how important meta data (e.g., names of authors, DOI, dates, etc.), equations, subsection titles, etc. are for a scientific document page in your opinion
