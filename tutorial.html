Setting

Text quality directly impacts LLM performance. Unfortunately, there is no universal measure for text quality. Metrics like BLEU etc. were created for sentence-length translations - hardly able to compare multi-page scientific documents. Regardless, faced with two options most people have a good idea what we prefer. This websites elicits such preferences.

Imagine you hold a document page in your hand that is about to disintegrate. What text of the two presented to you *best* represents the textual content of that page?

Modes

Judge two kinds of pages. Either paragraph-wise or a full page.

  • *"I preferred one text but the other made it to the next round. Why?"* Choices are **pre-selected and hence not adaptive to user selection**. No reason to overthink this.
  • Is there a button to go back and revise choices? **No**
  • What parsers created the text snipptes? **Many**: Nougat, Marker, Grobid, PyMuPDF, pypdf. Occasionally, GPT-4, and tesseract.
  • I know the output format of a few parsers: what if I'm biased? **The text formatting was scrambled (e.g. insertion/deletion of `#`) to prevent biasing participants.**

Factors to consider üìå

  • Text dropped, added, or (drastically) misaligned
  • Mis-parsed domain-specific words (e.g. compiler ‚Üí composer)
  • Irregular formatting (e.g. excessive newlines, no whitespaces)
  • Scrambled LaTeX equations (e.g. \( \int_{0}^{\infty} f(y) \mbox{d}y \) ‚Üí So 8 f( xdx ]$)

Advice üìå

  • **Don't read but skim** Glance at subsection titles, paragraph beginnings/endings, exotic words.
  • Be principled but follow your gut: Preferences are not fixed rules otherwise we could code them up)
  • Judge, not study. It doesn't take domain knowledge to spot errors. You won't be quizzed on the content of these documents at the end.
